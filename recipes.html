Classic Red Velvet Cake

Chef Nat's go-to celebration cake. Dense, moist layers with a tangy cream cheese frosting.

Prep20 min
Bake30–35 min
Total~1 hr 20 min
Serves10–12
DifficultyMedium

Ingredients

  • 2½ cups (315g) cake flour
  • 1½ cups (300g) caster sugar
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp cocoa powder
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1½ cups (360ml) buttermilk
  • 1 cup (240ml) vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp red food colouring
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp white vinegar

Cream Cheese Frosting

  • 250g full-fat cream cheese, room temperature
  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 3 cups (360g) icing sugar, sifted
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Method

  1. Preheat your oven to 175°C. Grease and line two 23cm round cake tins.
  2. In a large bowl, sift together the flour, sugar, baking soda, salt and cocoa powder.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, buttermilk, oil, food colouring, vanilla and vinegar until well combined.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and fold gently until just combined — do not overmix.
  5. Divide the batter evenly between the two tins. Bake for 30–35 minutes or until a skewer inserted in the centre comes out clean.
  6. Leave the cakes to cool in the tins for 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack and cool completely before frosting.
  7. For the frosting: beat cream cheese and butter together until smooth and fluffy. Add icing sugar gradually, then vanilla. Beat until light and creamy.
  8. Sandwich the two layers with frosting, then coat the top and sides. Crumble any offcuts on top as decoration if desired.

Chef Nat's Tips

  • Room-temperature ingredients make a noticeably smoother batter — don't skip this.
  • Gel food colouring gives a deeper red than liquid without affecting the batter consistency.
  • Chill the frosted cake for 30 minutes before serving for cleaner slices.

Loaded Kota

A Soweto classic with attitude. The kota is everything — you make it your own.

Prep10 min
Cook20 min
Total30 min
Serves1–2
DifficultyEasy

Ingredients

  • 1 large white bread loaf (quarter cut — the kota)
  • 2 beef or chicken polony slices
  • 2 Russians (smoked sausage), sliced and pan-fried
  • 1 egg, fried to your liking
  • Handful of slap chips (thick-cut chips)
  • 2 tbsp Atchar (pickled mango or mixed)
  • 1 tbsp tomato sauce
  • 1 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 2–3 slices of cheese (processed or cheddar)
  • Salt & pepper to taste

Method

  1. Cut the quarter loaf diagonally, hollowing out a portion of the bread inside to make room for the fillings.
  2. Spread the inside with mayonnaise and tomato sauce.
  3. Layer in the polony, followed by the cheese slices.
  4. Add the pan-fried Russians on top.
  5. Pile in the slap chips generously — this is non-negotiable.
  6. Place the fried egg on top of the chips.
  7. Finish with a spoon of atchar for heat and tang. Adjust to your spice preference.
  8. Press the bread closed gently, wrap in a sheet of paper and serve immediately.

Chef Nat's Tips

  • The atchar is what makes or breaks a kota — don't skip it or be shy with it.
  • Want more heat? Add a spoon of peri-peri or a chilli sauce of your choice.
  • The chips must go in hot. A cold kota is a sad kota.
